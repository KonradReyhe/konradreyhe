## 4) Market notes to remember

- In DACH, average freelance hourly rates are ~**104 €/h** (freelancermap 2025)
- Competition is strongest for generic web dev
- Competition is **weaker** for pragmatic automation/internal tools
- Use "efficiency with AI" as a **delivery advantage**, NOT as the product

---

## 5) Legal & compliance guardrails (Germany/EU)

### Employment separation (CRITICAL)
- Never use employer code, data, customer names, internal docs, or internal prompts
- Work only outside working hours
- Keep total weekly hours reasonable (48h limit across all jobs)
- Keep clean separation of devices/accounts: no employer laptop, no employer accounts
- Avoid clients in employer's industry (energy sector) to prevent competition conflicts

### VAT/Invoicing
- **B2B EU clients:** Reverse-charge; invoice must include "Steuerschuldnerschaft des Leistungsempfängers"
- **B2B non-EU clients:** Often no German VAT; validate customer business status with evidence
- Consider Kleinunternehmerregelung initially (< 22.000€/year threshold)

### Before first invoice
- Register Gewerbe (Einzelunternehmen, Nebenerwerb)
- Consider informing employer with neutral description

## 6) Standard acquisition workflow (do not deviate)

```
1. Define micro-niche target
   └─ e.g., "SMBs drowning in Excel + manual reporting"

2. Build 1-page landing page
   ├─ 1 sentence value prop
   ├─ 3 services
   ├─ 2 example outcomes (time saved)
   └─ CTA: "Book 20 min call"

3. Create portfolio folder
   ├─ 2 anonymized mini case studies (problem → solution → impact)
   └─ 1 technical credibility page (stack, deployment, security basics)

4. Outreach
   ├─ 30 targets in DACH (LinkedIn + email + local)
   └─ Keep pitch short; offer a pilot

5. Discovery call
   └─ Qualify: problem severity, data access, budget, timeline

6. Proposal
   └─ Fixed-price pilot (1–2 weeks) + optional monthly maintenance

7. Delivery
   └─ Fast iteration, documentation, handover, testimonial
```
